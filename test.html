Introduction

Before jumping into the database you may want to learn a few things about Cherry. How the brand came to be, a quick rundown of why we love Cherry keyboards, what they are exactly, what we know about their nomenclature, how to date their keyboards and some specifications about the database.

Cherry GmbH

Cherry was founded in 1953 in the USA by Walter Cherry. At the beginning, Cherry was only making microswtiches, it's only in 1973 that they began production of computer keyboards and computer keyboard dedicated switches. Notably with the introduction of the M4, M5 and M6 switches (later regrouped under the M7 switch line).

Since then they have introduced the M8, M9, MX (X as in 10 in roman numerals), M11, ML, MY, Viola, MX Low Profile, MX Ultra Low Profile and a solid-state capacitive switch design. Each having multiple variations, depending on their characteristics.

In the early 1980s, Cherry expanded towards Europe by implementing a factory in Auerbach, Germany. From then henceforth, Cherry Germany and Cherry USA differentiated themselves from one another through their keyboards' article numbers and even switches.
